Biography

My research examines how educational choices, institutions, and public policies shape inequality over the life cycle, with a particular focus on gender gaps in STEM, school-to-work transitions, and labor market outcomes. I use administrative and survey data, randomized field experiments, and quasi-experimental methods to conduct policy-relevant research in labor economics, education economics, gender inequality, and applied microeconometrics.

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Economics at the Universitat de Barcelona (expected 09/2026), where my dissertation — Essays on Gender Inequality, Educational Choices, and Labor Market Pathways — is supervised by Profs. Lídia Farré and Andreu Arenas.

Policy & Practice

Alongside my academic research, I have engaged directly with public institutions and practitioners to design, implement, and communicate policy-relevant evidence, including field implementation of randomized evaluations with vulnerable populations, a research–practice partnership with a large U.S. school district, and the design of a government-coordinated school-based RCT in Catalonia.

01/2023 – Present · Barcelona, Spain

School-based RCT in Catalonia

Institut d'Economia de Barcelona, Universitat de Barcelona

Designing and preparing the implementation of a school-based randomized information intervention in secondary schools in Catalonia, funded by the Women and Science Program at Paris Dauphine–PSL and developed in coordination with the Generalitat de Catalunya, aimed at reducing the gender gap in STEM. Lead day-to-day project development, including treatment design, survey design and implementation planning, school engagement, budget management, research assistant supervision, and coordination with schools and education stakeholders.

09/2018 – 09/2019 · Seattle, United States

Research–Practice Partnership with Seattle Public Schools

Education Policy Analytics Lab, College of Education, University of Washington

Worked in a research-practice partnership with Seattle Public Schools — the largest K–12 provider in Washington State — analyzing administrative data on teacher effectiveness, absenteeism, recruitment, and retention in high-poverty schools. Shaped empirical questions through ongoing dialogue with district executives, school administrators, union representatives, and program managers, and produced internal reports, technical notes, and presentations for senior district leadership.

06/2015 – 07/2018 · Montevideo, Uruguay

Field Implementation of Randomized Evaluations with Vulnerable Populations

Center for Research in Applied Economics, Universidad de Montevideo

Managed field implementation workstreams for randomized evaluations in Uruguay, including a social benefits take-up and labor inclusion intervention with more than 300 informal street workers and a digital inclusion intervention with over 120 institutionalized older adults in a public hospital. Designed and revised survey instruments and field protocols, supervised data collection, and coordinated day-to-day field activities with ministries, municipal departments, hospitals, frontline providers, and social workers.
